Ayurveda · Explained

What is abhyanga?

Abhyanga (pronounced ub-yun-gah) is the traditional ayurvedic full-body massage performed with warm herbal oils. It's one of the oldest and most widely practiced treatments in ayurvedic medicine — used for centuries as a daily and occasional ritual for circulation, nervous system balance and skin nourishment.

What makes it different from a regular massage

The oil is the point. Before your session, the oil is gently warmed — this is what separates abhyanga from a standard dry or lightly-oiled massage. Warm oil penetrates the skin more easily, softens the tissue before deeper work begins, and stays warm throughout the session, which is part of why abhyanga tends to feel deeply calming rather than purely physical.

The strokes are long and rhythmic, following the direction of circulation, with more focused attention at the joints. It's a full-body treatment — head to feet — rather than targeted spot work, though it can still release specific areas of tension along the way.

What it's used for

  • Circulation. The warm oil and rhythmic strokes support blood flow.
  • Nervous system. The steady pace and warmth are grounding — many people describe it as the most relaxing treatment they've had.
  • Skin. Warm herbal oils nourish and soften the skin as part of the massage itself.
  • Muscular tension. Combined with firmer work, it also releases tension the way a conventional massage would.

It's a wellness and relaxation treatment, not a medical one — a good fit for anyone who wants a genuinely restorative hour, whether that's after weeks of travel, a stretch of poor sleep, or just because it's been too long since you slowed down.
