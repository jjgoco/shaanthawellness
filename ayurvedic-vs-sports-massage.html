Choosing a treatment

Ayurvedic vs. sports massage: which one do you need?

Both are real massage traditions with real technique behind them — they're just built to solve different problems. Here's the actual difference, so you can book the right one instead of guessing.

Ayurvedic massage (abhyanga)

Performed with warm herbal oils, in long, rhythmic, full-body strokes. It's a relaxation and nervous-system treatment first — the goal is to calm you down, support circulation and leave your skin nourished. See what is abhyanga for the full breakdown.

Best for: unwinding after travel, a stretch of poor sleep or stress, or simply wanting a genuinely restorative hour rather than targeted work.

Sports massage

Deep-tissue work, applied with more pressure and more precision to the areas that are actually tight — shoulders, lower back, calves, whatever your body is holding onto. The goal is recovery and function, not just relaxation.

Best for: athletes, surfers and anyone physically active who needs muscles to recover faster — see surf recovery massage in Sagres if that's you.

Side by side

  • Technique — ayurvedic: warm oil, full body, rhythmic. Sports: deep tissue, targeted, firmer pressure.
  • Goal — ayurvedic: relaxation, circulation, nervous system. Sports: muscle recovery, mobility, performance.
  • Feel afterwards — ayurvedic: calm, soft, unwound. Sports: looser, sometimes a little sore, more mobile.
  • Good for — ayurvedic: anyone wanting to slow down. Sports: anyone training, surfing or physically pushing their body.
